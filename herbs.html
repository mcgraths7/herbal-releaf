Herbs

CBD

CBD is an alternative to cannabis if you want to go the more natural way without getting “high” or you can even add CBD to your cannabis when smoking. The official term for CBD is cannabidiol and it is one of the many active compounds found in cannabis plants. CBD interacts with receptors in the central nervous system which causes calming effects. It also serves for many more things with the body such as pain-relieving and is an anti-inflammatory. CBD is used to treat various conditions such as seizures, pain, mental disorders, inflammatory bowel disease, nausea, migraine, depression, and anxiety.

Cannabis

There are over one hundred different cannabinoids and all are used for different reasons. For example, Sativa cannabinoids are more for sleep aid vs indica wakes you up and helps you get your to-do list done. Cannabinoids are naturally occurring compounds found in the cannabis plant. The main psychoactive ingredient in cannabis is a compound called delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (Δ9-THC), this is what makes you “high”. Certain cannabinoids are for chronic pain, PTSD, or other mental help. The THC molecules that pass through the blood-brain barrier will snuggly the receptors that receive compounds called endocannabinoids which the body self produces.

Natural Herbs

Natural herbs can also offer their own therapeutic or psychoactive effects, some shared with the same terpenes as cannabis. Mixing herbs with your cannabis enhances the flavor as well as complements the other effects or new ones. For example, smelling or bathing in lavender helps you relax. The same results happen when smoked. Just like drinking chamomile tea makes you sleepy, smoking chamomile tea will make you tired. Dejanae Evins, a Miss Grass favorite weed expert states, “Mixing smokable herb blends into your cannabis ritual really emphasizes how expansive and creative we can be in the way we approach self-care.” It is very important to state that inhaling anything into your body can potentially harm your body so be smart. When choosing herbs they should be organically-grown, food-grade herbs and if possible ethically-raised
